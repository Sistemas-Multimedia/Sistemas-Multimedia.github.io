1 Description

1.1 Intra (I) video coding

In the III... (or Intra video) coding, the 2D block-DWT, the 2D DWT, or any other spatial transform, is used on sequences of frames (images) to exploit the spatial correlation. This is achieved by simply iterating the spatial decorrelation as it is described in the Algorithm 1 [1], where V in the input sequence and S controls the number of SRLs (Spatial Resolution Levels)1 . The synthesis transform is computed using the Algorithm 2. In the Fig. 1 there is an example of the decomposition generated for three frames V 0, V 1 and V 2.

Algorithm 1: III-coding(V /* original video sequence */, S /* Number of extra levels */) → (O /* transformed video sequence */)

  1. O = {} /* empty sequence */.

  2. for Vi ∈V:

    1. Oi ←2D-TS(Vi) /* 2D analysis spatial transform */.

Algorithm 2: III-decoding(O /* transformed video sequence */, S /* Number of extra levels */) → (V /* original video sequence */)

  1. V = {} /* empty sequence */.

  2. for Oi ∈O:

    1. Vi ←2D-T−S(Oi) /* 2D synthesis spatial transform */.


Figure 1: Decomposition generated by 1-levels (S = 1) 2D-DWT and the 2x2-DCT (block size is equal to S + 1).

1.2 Motion compensation prediction (P) video coding

It’s time to test the performance of the ME/MC process previously described. We will encode a sequence of frames {Wk} using the pattern IPP..., which means that the ﬁrst frame will be intra-coded (I-type frame) and the rest of frames of the GOF (Group Of Frames) will be predicted-coded (P-type frame), respect to the previous one.


Figure 2: A simple IPP... image codec.

IPP... coding can be done by the codec shown in the Fig. 2, where (for the case of the YCoCg transform):

V k ←C(Wk) = [ 1 4 1 2 1 4 1 2 0 −1 2 −1 4 1 2 −1 4 ] [ Wk.R Wk.G Wk.B ] , (a)
Z−1(V k) = V k−1, (b)

and by deﬁnition, Z−1(V −1) = 0,

V k → k − 1 ←M(V k,V k−1), (c)

where M stands for Motion Estimation, and by deﬁnition, V 0 → (−1) = 0,

V k → k − 1 ∼ ←EV →(V k → k − 1), (d)

where EV →(⋅) represents the lossy compression of the motion data,

V k → k − 1 ∼ ←DV →(V k → k − 1 ∼), (e)

where DV →(⋅) represents the decompression of the motion data,

Ek ← V k −V ∧k, (f)

where the symbol − represents to the pixel-wise substraction,

Ek ∼ ←EE(Ek), (g)

where EE(⋅) represents the lossy compression of the prediction error texture data,

E ∼k ←DE(E ∼k), (h)

where DE(⋅) represents the decompression of the prediction error texture data,

V ∼k ←E ∼k + V ∧k, (i)

and notice that if V ∧k = 0, then E ∼k = V ∼k,

V ∧k ←P(V k → k − 1 ∼,V ∼k−1), (j)

where P(⋅,⋅) is a motion compensated predictor.

Notice that if V ∧k is similar to V k, then Ek will be approximately zero, and therefore, easely compressed. Another interesting aspect to highlight is that the encoder replicates de decoder in order to use the reconstructed images as reference and avoid the drift error.
