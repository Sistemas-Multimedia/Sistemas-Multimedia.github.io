1 Description

It’s time to test the performance of the ME/MC process previously described, in the image domain. We encode a sequence of frames {Wk} using the pattern IPP... which means that the ﬁrst frame will be intra-coded (I-type frame) and the rest of frames of the GOF (Group Of (Frames) will be predicted-coded (P-type frame), respect to the previous one.

1.1 A block diagram of the step codec


Figure 1: A simple IPP... image codec.

The IPP... coding can be done by the codec shown in the Fig. 1, where:

V k ←C(Wk) = [ 1 4 1 2 1 4 1 2 0 −1 2 −1 4 1 2 −1 4 ] [ Wk.R Wk.G Wk.B ] , (a)
Z−1(V k) = V k−1, (b)

and by deﬁnition, Z−1(V −1) = 0,

V k → k − 1 ←M(V k,V k−1), (c)

where M stands for Motion Estimation, and by deﬁnition, V 0 → (−1) = 0,

V k → k − 1 ∼ ←EV →(V k → k − 1), (d)

where EV →(⋅) represents the lossy compression of the motion data,

V k → k − 1 ∼ ←DV →(V k → k − 1 ∼), (e)

where DV →(⋅) represents the decompression of the motion data,

Ek ← V k −V ∧k, (f)

where the symbol − represents to the pixel-wise substraction,

Ek ∼ ←EE(Ek), (g)

where EE(⋅) represents the lossy compression of the prediction error texture data,

E ∼k ←DE(E ∼k), (h)

where DE(⋅) represents the decompression of the prediction error texture data,

V ∼k ←E ∼k + V ∧k, (i)

and notice that if V ∧k = 0 then V ∼k = [E ∼k,

Notice that if V ∧k is similar to V k, then Ek will be approximately zero, and therefore, easely compressed.
