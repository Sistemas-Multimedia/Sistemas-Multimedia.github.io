1 Objective

This task shows how to implement a very simple lossy (gray-scale) image compressor [1] based on Scalar Quantization [4, 5, 6] and PNG [3]. This compressor basically removes the number of different gray tones in a single-channel image (normally, humans hardly distinguish more than 64 different colors). Therefore, the main objective of this task is to analyze the impact in the encoding performance (in terms of a RD (Rate/Distortion) curve [2]) when the visual redundancy is exploited.

2 Scalar quantization applied to the “intensity” domain of a gray-scale image

The notetooks gray_SQ_deadzone.ipynb, gray_SQ_LloydMax.ipynb, gray_SQ_companded.ipynb, gray_SQ_midrise.ipynb, gray_SQ_midtread.ipynb and compare_quantizers.ipynb) shows the RD performance of different scalar quantizers. As you can see, the way in which the visual information is quantized has an influence in the RD performance. Also, as it can be checked in the notebooks, in general, the better quantizer is the Lloyd-Max quantizer because it adapts the QSSs (Quantization Step Sizes) to the PDF (Probability Density Function) of the image. However, notice also that, since the QSS depends on the image, they must be “transmitted” to the decoder, which decreases the definitive RD performance.

Notice also the remarkable perormance of the DeadZone Quantizer (DZQ) at low bit-rates. This is one of the reasons why the DZQ is used in several image and video codec. Another reason is that, when the QSSs are powers of two, we can use an bit-plane-based entropy encoder.

Finally, notice that (you must see the notebooks) when the QSSs are powers of 2, the points of the RD curve tend to belong to the convex hull. On the contrary, if the QSSs can be any, the RD curves can have points for bit-rates \(i\) and \(i+1\) where the condition \(\lambda _i\ge \lambda _{i+1}\) (being \(\lambda _i\) the slope of the RD curve at the bit-rate \(i\)) is not satisfied, which can difficult the (bit-)rate control.
