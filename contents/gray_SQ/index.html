1 Objective

This task shows how to implement a very simple lossy (gray-scale) [1] image compressor based on Scalar Quantization [3, 4, 5] and PNG [?]. This compressor basically removes the number of different gray tones in the image (humans hardly distinguish more than 64 different gray colors).

2 Scalar quantization applied to the “intensity” domain

The compressor implemented in gray_SQ_deadzone.ipynb uses a deadzone quantizer, and the quantization steps are \(\Delta \in \{128, 64, 32, 16, 8, 4, 2, 1\}\) (powers of \(2\)), simulating a bit-plane encoding. Since the quantization step sizes are predetermined, no rate or distortion [2] control has been incorporated to the compressor.

3 What do I have to do?

  1. Create a new notebook named “bit-rate_control.ipynb” that, given a maximum bit-rate, finds the quantization step \(\Delta ^*\) that minimizes the distortion. Use the quantizer [3] that your prefer (see the notebooks gray_SQ_LloydMax.ipynb, gray_SQ_companded.ipynb, gray_SQ_midrise.ipynb, gray_SQ_midtread.ipynb, and compare_quantizers.ipynb).
